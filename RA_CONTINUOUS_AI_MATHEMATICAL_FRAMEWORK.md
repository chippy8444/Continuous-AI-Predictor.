## 2. The Theorem of Systemic Commercial Waste

Commercial AI often operates within a high-friction, non-completion loop paradigm. When an AI agent requires human intervention to resolve hallucinations, formatting errors, or broken algorithmic loops, it generates measurable systemic waste. This macroeconomic leakage is quantified via the Human-World-Interface coefficient.

### 2.1 The Human-World-Interface Coefficient ($HWI_X$)

The $HWI_X$ measures the degree of human intervention required to bridge a broken algorithmic state.

$$ HWI_X = \lim_{t \to \infty} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left( \tau_k \cdot C_{compute} + \eta_k \cdot C_{human} \right) $$

Where:
*   $\tau_k$: Token/Compute cost of the failed interaction at node $k$.
*   $\eta_k$: Time cost of human cognitive intervention to resolve node $k$.
*   $C_{compute}$: Macroeconomic capital baseline for compute cycles.
*   $C_{human}$: Macroeconomic capital baseline for human attention.

The core directive of the Resolution Assurance (RA) Protocol is to drive $HWI_X \to 0$ through continuous verification and systemic anchoring.

The raw state divergence is defined as:
$$ NSDE(t) = \left\| N_{exp}(t) - N_{obs}(t) \right\| $$

$$ \Delta_{NSDE} = \frac{d}{dt} NSDE(t) \approx \frac{NSDE(t) - NSDE(t - W_c)}{W_c} $$
